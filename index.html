Officially an Amazonian!

December 5, 2021

I just started working at Amazon. It’s refreshing to be back on the payroll, but even more so to be working with people again. I forgot how organized and procedural the American system is when it comes to work. I’ve literally not worked in this country for the last 5 years, while being abroad in China but more on that later. To clarify, I work as a warehouse associate packing and inspecting your guys’ packages! Making sure your items aren’t damaged and if it is, reporting it in a timely manner. There’s a whole gang of employees working in different departments of the warehouse. It’s fascinating to see how everything operates inside. That’s the biggest reason I took on this job.

It’s training week, but so far everything from cleanliness, lighting, coloring, safety precautions and general work environment vibe checks out. The atmosphere in there is a bunch of young adults aged from 18-34 and to keep things lively and not so dull and repetitive, the warehouse is blasted with 90s classic jams. It almost feels like a club in there.

I only realized during the orientation how much benefits Amazon employees receives. They have an incentive where if you work with them for a year full-time, you get qualified for a tuition reimbursement of up to $12-13k with the assistance of placing you in a career track. The absurd amount of PTO they give you, even for part-time employees like myself, is another cool perk.

In all honesty, one of the first things I thought of stepping inside with my yellow vest, gloves and protective gear -- not to mention all the delivery sprinter vans lined up outside of the warehouse -- was Squid Game.

Catch you guys in the next one, see ya!

First post of 2022..

February 23, 2022

Wow -- This word has stuck with me since childhood and it's an expression you can never go wrong with.

There's a lot going on leading up to this part of the post alone. Let's break it down a bit. It's been nearly 3 months since I last pushed a blog post and since then, I quit Amazon -- only lasted there for a few weeks. Didn't really feel like working during the holidays, and that's exactly what I did -- not work and celebrate with family. Well worth it, but my credit cards say otherwise.

I now work for Revature! A company that chews you up for little pay and moves you around the country where need be (after training). I'm in training right now and boy, is it starting to really feel like hell -- deadlines, weekly assessments, weekly oral interview-style test questions about what you've learned in the past week, coding projects and I'm stressing over the first one due in one week from now.

Work is fully remote and now I can see why Americans have fallen in love with this "work-environment." It's almost too comforting, but I hope I'm able to move with Revature and actually go out the east of this country. And while I might not be explaining too much about the company I work for, that isn't really the point of this post. It's really just to rant as to what's happened with my code and this blog in the last few months.

This project that's due in a month is critical to this training program and I'm just really hoping to perform well and make my code the most functional it could be. I'm coding in Java and it's all back-end logic. And here I was thinking I'm going to be a front-end developer. What a sudden change of life events in just these few months alone. I didn't really have to shotgun approach the application process, since Revature reached out, made me take an assesment, and in January I settled all the paper to hit the ground running the first of Feb.

That's all I really have for tonight, and I can't forget these newly-create principles to live by, and that's to commit my code a lot more often. We live in a world where our social media presence is tracked and surprisingly enough, you can still do that in a positive manner, which I'm trying my best to practice.
