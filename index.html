It's Just One of Those Days

March 10, 2022

If the title of this post isn't anymore of a giveaway to how this day is going.

It literally is just one of those off days, though, and it's in these instances where one has to weather the storm. I might have cheated myself and had a smash burger, but I'm glad that I'm a lot more aware of these situations and surprisingly a lot more collected than my former self. Pat on the back for that.

I've participated into this Lent season. First one since I can remember, honestly, I can't even tell you when the last time I've gone to ash wednesday. If there is one lesson I've learned about this season of lent so far, that's the sacrifices you've got to make. We all have to make sacrfices every once in a while.

Let's start this sacrifice I'm making by attempting to work in the dining room at 19:08 on a Thursday. The television is on. Jeopardy is playing. Everyone is going about their own thing. Eating dinner, random but loud background noises. You know this is the same dining room where I'd do my homework in my adolescent days. Now I have a better understanding as to why I was always distracted. These noises will serve any human as a distraction. Just weather the storm. Focus harder and get some work in.

Speaking of work, that's all I do. My code didn't run today and thankfully, only a part of it. The feeling of your code not running is never a pleasant one. I wish I caught the bug early on during work, but that didn't happen. It's alright, no big deal. We'll figure it out later.

It's the 6th week of Revature's training, and the following weeks to come are (as I expect) to be the hardest and most gruelling. Complex projects but the front end is finally included, and that's more of a relief than a burden. The funny thing is: I expected to work more with the front-end after Coding Dojo's program, but all I've pretty much done til this point is all back-end logic. Endless conditional statements. I'm trying to find time on the side for more practice, but there's always a catchup game to be played. This week is to drill 3 weeks worth of concepts into my head for next week's Quality Control check. Fun.

It's funny how when you're stressed, it seems like it's only happening to you, but I'm sure that my batch mates also feel the struggle of keeping up with the intensity. Breathe, pals. We're gonna get through this one day and one line of code at a time. That's pretty much it for tonight's Time for another night of memory drills, which is what most of this weekend will look like. In the mean time, stay easy.

Officially an Amazonian!

December 5, 2021

I just started working at Amazon. It’s refreshing to be back on the payroll, but even more so to be working with people again. I forgot how organized and procedural the American system is when it comes to work. I’ve literally not worked in this country for the last 5 years, while being abroad in China but more on that later. To clarify, I work as a warehouse associate packing and inspecting your guys’ packages! Making sure your items aren’t damaged and if it is, reporting it in a timely manner. There’s a whole gang of employees working in different departments of the warehouse. It’s fascinating to see how everything operates inside. That’s the biggest reason I took on this job.

It’s training week, but so far everything from cleanliness, lighting, coloring, safety precautions and general work environment vibe checks out. The atmosphere in there is a bunch of young adults aged from 18-34 and to keep things lively and not so dull and repetitive, the warehouse is blasted with 90s classic jams. It almost feels like a club in there.

I only realized during the orientation how much benefits Amazon employees receives. They have an incentive where if you work with them for a year full-time, you get qualified for a tuition reimbursement of up to $12-13k with the assistance of placing you in a career track. The absurd amount of PTO they give you, even for part-time employees like myself, is another cool perk.

In all honesty, one of the first things I thought of stepping inside with my yellow vest, gloves and protective gear -- not to mention all the delivery sprinter vans lined up outside of the warehouse -- was Squid Game.

Catch you guys in the next one, see ya!
