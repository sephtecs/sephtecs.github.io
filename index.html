Officially an Amazonian!

December 5, 2021

I just started working at Amazon. It’s refreshing to be back on the payroll, but even more so to be working with people again. I forgot how organized and procedural the American system is when it comes to work. I’ve literally not worked in this country for the last 5 years, while being abroad in China but more on that later. To clarify, I work as a warehouse associate packing and inspecting your guys’ packages! Making sure your items aren’t damaged and if it is, reporting it in a timely manner. There’s a whole gang of employees working in different departments of the warehouse. It’s fascinating to see how everything operates inside. That’s the biggest reason I took on this job.

It’s training week, but so far everything from cleanliness, lighting, coloring, safety precautions and general work environment vibe checks out. The atmosphere in there is a bunch of young adults aged from 18-34 and to keep things lively and not so dull and repetitive, the warehouse is blasted with 90s classic jams. It almost feels like a club in there.

I only realized during the orientation how much benefits Amazon employees receives. They have an incentive where if you work with them for a year full-time, you get qualified for a tuition reimbursement of up to $12-13k with the assistance of placing you in a career track. The absurd amount of PTO they give you, even for part-time employees like myself, is another cool perk.

In all honesty, one of the first things I thought of stepping inside with my yellow vest, gloves and protective gear -- not to mention all the delivery sprinter vans lined up outside of the warehouse -- was Squid Game.

Catch you guys in the next one, see ya!
